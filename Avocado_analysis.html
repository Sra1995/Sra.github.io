Avo-Data Extravaganza

Objective

To conduct a thorough exploration and analysis of avocado sales data, revealing key patterns, regional variations, and pricing trends. By employing data visualization techniques, this project aims to offer meaningful insights into the dynamics of avocado consumption and market behavior.

Based on dataset I obtained is seems that the volume of sales have been on small marginal increase of 400 million a year However, it seems that the price of avocados have been on a steady increase of 0.2 dollars a year on average for the conventional type(most dominate type purchased), which might affected this trend the trend declined to 100 million a year in 2017, and we lack data for 2018 and 2019 or 2014 and below, so we can't make a conclusion on the trend

Percentage Change Formula

Since the dataset I obtained only have reliable data from 2015 to 2017, I decided to use the percentage change formula to calculate the percentage change of price over the years from 2017 to 2017. 

  
# Calculate the percentage change column
average_price_by_location['Percentage Change'] = ((average_price_by_location[2017] / average_price_by_location[2015] - 1) * 100).apply(lambda x: f'{x:.2f}%')

The code above is the code I used to calculate the percentage change of price over the years from 2017 to 2015.

Average Volume Sales per year

The graph above shows the average volume sales per year. The graph shows that the average volume sales per year is increasing annually. Also shows that the average volume sales per year is the highest in 2018.

Avocados are available year round like most agricultural commodities these days, but January through March is the best time of year for flavor. It is during this time that the fruit has developed higher oil content, resulting in that buttery flavor and texture that we all love. Hence, why the average volume sales per year is the highest in 2018 decpite data only having just partial Q1 data for 2018.
